Frequently Asked Questions

Who can donate blood? ▼
Generally, you can donate blood if you are at least 17 years old, weigh at least 50 kg, and are in good health. You must not have donated blood in the last 3 months.
How often can I donate blood? ▼
Whole blood can typically be donated every 3 months (56 days). The system will automatically track your next eligible donation date.
What happens during the screening process? ▼
You will first complete a self-assessment form online. On donation day, a medical staff member will check your vital signs including blood pressure, pulse, hemoglobin level, and weight before approving you for donation.
How do I track my donation history? ▼
Log in to your Donor Dashboard and click the "Donation History" tab to view all your past donations and statuses.

Blood Donation Policies

Donor Eligibility Policy

All donors must meet minimum health and age requirements set by the Department of Health (DOH) and JRMMC. Donors who fail the self-assessment or physical screening will not be permitted to donate during that session.

Data Privacy Policy

All personal and medical information collected through Mapúa RedTrace is governed by the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act 10173). Donor information is used solely for the management of the blood donation program and will not be shared with unauthorized third parties.

Appointment and Scheduling Policy

Donors are required to complete the self-assessment form before their scheduled donation appointment. Walk-in donations may be accepted subject to staff availability and inventory needs.

Blood Inventory and Usage Policy

Collected blood units are stored and managed by JRMMC in accordance with national blood banking standards. Blood units are allocated based on medical priority and request status (Pending / Approved / Released).
